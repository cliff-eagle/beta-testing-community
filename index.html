Study Overview

Human-Centered Acquisition.

While current educational technology excels at the delivery of discrete linguistic units, it often fails to provide the social and environmental conditions necessary for genuine acquisition. This study investigates the "Fluency Paradox"—where learners demonstrate app-based competence but remain functionally unable to participate in authentic interactions.

Our work focuses on "Socially-Situated Simulations"—environments where learners must navigate cultural norms, tone, and interpersonal pressure to succeed. Your role as a tester is to evaluate the fidelity and pedagogical effectiveness of these interactions.

Pedagogical Foundations

Grounding our community design in established educational psychology and linguistic theory.

Pillar 01

Affective Filter

Krashen (1982)

Lowering performance anxiety through low-stakes, simulated social interactions to enable deep acquisition.

Pillar 02

Zone of Proximal Dev.

Vygotsky (1978)

Deploying dynamic AI scaffolding to keep learners at the precise edge of their emerging competence.

Pillar 03

Communicative Comp.

Hymes (1972)

Prioritizing socio-linguistic appropriateness and "perceived" fluency over discrete grammatical accuracy.

Pillar 04

Situated Learning

Lave & Wenger (1991)

Engaging in legitimate peripheral participation within persistent, context-rich community simulations.
