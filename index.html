Study Overview

Human-Centered Acquisition.

Traditional EdTech excels at content delivery but lacks the environmental nuance required for genuine fluency. This study focuses on "socially-situated simulations"—environments where learners must navigate cultural norms and tone to succeed.

Our objective is to evaluate how automated dialogic agents provide scaffolded support in real-time. By participating, you are contributing to a dataset that will inform inclusive, high-utility learning tools for global education and crisis management.

Pedagogical Foundations

Validating established theory through modern technical implementation.

Pillar 01

The Affective Filter

Investigating how simulated "low-stakes" environments bypass the performance anxiety associated with adult language acquisition. (Krashen, 1982)

Pillar 02

Zone of Proximal Dev.

Analyzing how algorithmic agents identify the learner's "edge of competence" to provide dynamic scaffolding. (Vygotsky, 1978)

Pillar 03

Communicative Comp.

Testing the efficacy of embodied practice over discrete grammar memorization in high-fidelity social scenarios. (Hymes, 1972)
