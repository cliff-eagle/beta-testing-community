Study Overview

Closing the Fluency Paradox.

Our research addresses the systemic failure in language technology: the inability to move from decontextualized digital practice to high-stakes social reality.

By engaging with our Socially-Situated Simulations, you provide the qualitative telemetry required to validate recursive AI scaffolding and affective filter reduction.

Study Metrics

Socio-Linguistic Appropriateness

Measuring tone, register, and cultural nuance vs grammar.

Cognitive Friction Analysis

Identifying points where scaffolding fails to support ZPD.

Pedagogical Foundations

The Four Research Pillars

01

Affective Filter

Krashen (1982)

Reducing performance anxiety via low-stakes, simulated social pressure environments.

02

Zone of Proximal Dev.

Vygotsky (1978)

Implementing recursive AI scaffolding at the precise edge of learner competence.

03

Communicative Comp.

Hymes (1972)

Shifting focus from discrete grammar to culturally situated social appropriateness.

04

Situated Learning

Lave & Wenger (1991)

Facilitating acquisition through legitimate peripheral participation in simulations.
